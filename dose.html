Logic or Emotion, Is There A Winner?

By: Terry Roy - Published on March 14, 2025

Lets get personal on a beautiful Friday. There is a war inside me. A constant, relentless battle between my heart and my mind, each screaming for dominance, neither willing to surrender. My heart pulls me toward passion, toward risk, toward the things that make me feel alive, even if they have no logic, no guarantee, no safety net. My mind, steady and calculating, warns me of consequence, reminds me of past mistakes, urges me to think twice before leaping into the unknown.

I have spent nights staring at the ceiling, paralyzed by the war between them. My heart whispers, Go for it. Chase it. Feel it. This is what you want. And for a moment, I almost do. But then my mind, colder, sharper, steps in with a question I can’t ignore: But at what cost? That is where the battle begins—the fight between what I want and what makes sense, between what sets my soul on fire and what keeps my life intact.

I have felt this war in ambition, in the choices that shape my future. My heart dreams big, sees possibility everywhere, believes in the impossible. It tells me to take the leap, to trust the unknown, to choose the path that excites me even if it doesn’t make sense to anyone else. My mind, ever the strategist, brings out the numbers, the probabilities, the risks. It reminds me that failure is real, that security matters, that not every dream is meant to be chased. My heart sees that as fear. My mind sees it as wisdom. And I stand in between, trying to decide which voice to trust.

There have been moments when I followed my heart and lost. Times when emotion blinded me, when I leapt without thinking, when I ran toward something that was never meant for me. There have been moments when my mind was right, when logic saved me, when reason kept me from making a mistake I couldn’t undo. But there have also been times when my heart knew something my mind couldn’t grasp. Times when I walked away from something practical because I knew, deep down, that it wasn’t for me. Times when logic told me no, but my soul said yes, and I listened, and it led me exactly where I was meant to be.

This war will never end. The mind will always fight for control, and the heart will always fight for freedom. The hardest part is knowing that neither is entirely right or entirely wrong. That sometimes, you have to trust the fire inside you, and sometimes, you have to be strong enough to let it burn out. That sometimes, you have to take the risk, and sometimes, you have to walk away. That sometimes, following your heart will lead to the greatest moments of your life, and sometimes, it will shatter you. And sometimes, ignoring it will be the biggest mistake you ever make.

I don’t know if I will ever master the balance. I don’t know if I will ever fully trust one without questioning the other. But that’s what it means to be human—to stand in the middle of this war, feeling the pull of both, knowing that the only real answer is the one I can live with. That the fight itself is the point. That the answer isn’t about choosing between logic and emotion, but about learning to live with both. That life isn’t about silencing one voice, but about learning which one to listen to, and when.
